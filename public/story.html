Memphis Kee's Story

Look, no one wants to read a long, pretentious band bio talking about who influenced me to play my first G chord in 1994 (Radiohead) or how I was in the George Jones Fan Club at age 12, so let’s keep it to the point.

I am a singer/songwriter/band leader that goes by the name Memphis Kee. I grew up in (you guessed it) Memphis, TN. I started playing music there and came to Austin, TX in 2002 and fell in love with the place. I served as a police officer and detective for 10 years and now own and operate a private investigation firm. It makes for excellent source material for songs. As a solo artist or full band, I/we have provided tour support for acts such as Jason Boland and The Stragglers, Pat Green, Stoney LaRue, Micky and The Motorcars, John Moreland, The Bellamy Brothers, Chris Knight, Brandon Rhyder, and more.

The new EP, “6th & San Jacinto” is my debut record as the front man. Its 5 songs that hit all the notes along the Country/Americana trail from brutally honest murder ballad to ears-back full-tilt honky tonk. The record was mixed/mastered by Adam Odor at Yellow Dog Studios in Wimberley, TX and features Mike and The Moonpies’ Omar Oyoque on steel guitar, internationally touring blues guitarist/singer Jordan Matthew Young on lead, and my full-time drummer, Caleb Kelly (formerly or Granger Smith) on drums.
